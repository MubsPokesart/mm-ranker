## Design Context

### Users
Members of the Historical NBA Drafts Discord community — basketball fans who participate in fantasy drafts of historical NBA single-season peaks. They access this tool via a shared Discord link to rank March Madness teams through pairwise comparisons. They're engaged hobbyists who care about the content (team rosters, player values) more than flashy UI. They use this on desktop and mobile, often while chatting in Discord.

### Brand Personality
**Precise. Quiet. Functional.**

This is a tool, not a product. It should feel like infrastructure — present when needed, invisible otherwise. The interface earns trust through clarity and restraint.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Reference:** Cursor IDE and cursor.com — deep blacks, monochrome hierarchy through opacity, ultra-subtle borders, clean whitespace
- **Theme:** Light mode. Warm off-white backgrounds (`#f7f7f4`), near-black text (`#26251e`).
- **Color philosophy:** Almost entirely monochrome. Black text on white backgrounds. The ONLY color comes from team card headers (region-specific colors) — these are the content, not the chrome. Accent blue (`#2b5ea7`) used sparingly for primary actions only.
- **Anti-references:** Glassmorphism, gradient text, neon accents, card shadows, bouncy animations, hero sections with metrics, generic SaaS landing pages. Nothing that looks AI-generated.

### Design Principles

1. **Monochrome hierarchy** — Use opacity (not color) to create visual hierarchy. Primary text at 92% white, muted at 55%, dim at 26%. Borders at 7% white. Color is reserved for team identity and critical actions only.

2. **Let the content breathe** — Generous whitespace. No decorative elements. The team rosters ARE the visual interest — the UI frames them, doesn't compete.

3. **Invisible interactions** — Transitions are fast (150ms), subtle (opacity/transform only), and functional. No bouncing, no spring physics, no delight-for-delight's-sake. If you removed an animation and nothing felt worse, it shouldn't have been there.

4. **Flat and honest** — No shadows, no gradients, no faux-depth. Surfaces are differentiated by value alone: `#f7f7f4` → `#ffffff` → `#ededea`. Borders are barely visible.

5. **Dense but not cramped** — Team cards show 10 players in a tight spreadsheet grid because density serves the comparison task. But controls (buttons, navigation) get breathing room. Match information density to the user's task at each point.
